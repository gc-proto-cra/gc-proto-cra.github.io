The normal period for individuals and graduated rate estates is:

  • within three years of the end of the tax year to claim a tax refund
  • within three years of the date of the original notice of assessment to request a reassessment

If your income tax return(s) were not previously filed, individuals and graduated rate estates may request a refund by filing them. Send the income tax returns and supporting documentation to the tax office where you file your returns.

  • Individuals: visit Tax services offices and tax centres for addresses
  • Graduated rate estates: visit Where to file a T3 return for addresses

If your income tax return(s) was/were previously filed:

  • For individual accounts, go to How to change your return for instructions on submitting your adjustment request.
  • For graduated rate estates, send us a completed Form T3-ADJ, T3 Adjustment Request, or letter providing the details of the change. Indicate the trust’s account number, the tax year you want to change, and attach all supporting documents. For details about where to send your letter or completed form, see Form T3-ADJ, T3 Adjustment Request.

The normal period for a corporation:

  • to file a return and claim a tax refund is within three years of the end of the tax year.

The normal period for a corporation to request a reassessment:

  • is within three years of the date we sent the original notice of assessment for the tax year, if the corporation was a Canadian-controlled private corporation (CCPC) at the end of the year
  • is within four years if it was not a CCPC at the end of the year.
  • Note: this normal reassessment period may be extended a further 3 years in certain situations: see Extended reassessment period

See Requesting a reassessment of your T2 return for details on how to submit a reassessment request beyond the normal period.

See Re-appropriation of T2 statute-barred credits for details on how to claim a tax refund beyond the normal period.

Inability to pay

The CRA may, in circumstances where there is a confirmed inability to pay amounts owing, consider waiving or cancelling interest in whole or in part to enable taxpayers to pay their account.

Consideration is not generally given to cancelling a penalty based on an inability to pay or financial hardship unless an extraordinary circumstance prevented compliance, or an exceptional situation existed.

For requests to cancel or waive interest or penalties based on an inability to pay or financial hardship, the CRA requires full financial disclosure from taxpayers, including a statement of income, expenses, assets, and liabilities.
