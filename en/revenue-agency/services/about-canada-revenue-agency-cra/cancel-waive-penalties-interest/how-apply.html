How to apply

You will need to prepare the following documentation to complete your request:

  • your social insurance number (SIN), account number, partnership number, trust account number, business number (BN), or any other identification number assigned to you by the CRA
  • the tax year(s) or fiscal period(s) involved
  • any documentation relevant to your qualifying situation:
    • Inability to pay or financial hardship

      You will need full financial disclosure including a statement of income, expenses, assets, and liabilities.

      • For individuals, see Statement of Income and Expenses and Assets and Liabilities for Individuals
      • For businesses, see Determining a business’ financial situation)
    • Incorrect information given by the CRA

      You will need supporting details of incorrect information given by the CRA in the form of written answers, published information or other evidence.

      When the incorrect information given by the CRA is of a verbal nature, you should give all possible details such as date, time, name of CRA official spoken to, and details of the conversation

  • a complete history of events including any measures that have been taken, e.g., payments and payment arrangements, and when they were taken to resolve the non-compliance
  • other documentation (if applicable), such as death certificates, doctor's statements, insurance statements

Submit your request

Online (personal)

For tax relief related to an individual, you can sign into My Account or Represent a client and use submit a request through the:

  • Request relief of penalties and interest service
Sign in to My Account Register

Alternative: Represent a client

Online (business)

Download and complete Form RC4288

  • Form RC4288, Request for Taxpayer Relief - Cancel or Waive Penalties or Interest

Then submit the form online in My Business Account using the:

  • Submit documents service
Sign in to My Business Account Register
By mail (personal or business)

Download and complete Form RC4288
