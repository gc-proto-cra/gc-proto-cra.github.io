After preparing a tax return

Volunteer responsibilities

To ensure the program's success, organizations and volunteers must safeguard taxpayer information and understand their responsibilities in this respect.

  • Do not use or disclose the individual's information for any purpose other than to complete their return
  • Store the individual's information securely in a locked drawer or cabinet and never leave their information unattended
  • Be mindful of your surroundings when speaking with individuals, especially in a virtual clinic setting when other people are present in your home or you have technological devices such as Google Home or Alexa
  • In the event of any type of security breach, such as lost, stolen or compromised data, tell your community organization and your Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) coordinator immediately

Once the tax return has been prepared and successfully transmitted, ensure you take the necessary steps to protect the individual’s personal information and provide them with correct information if adjustments are requested.

Return all documentation to the individual

Do not keep any of the individual’s documents (paper or electronic). This includes the completed TIS60 as well as any calculations or notes you might have made.

Clear all user information from UFile

If you work on a computer that you share with other volunteers within your organization, be sure you clear all of your user information at the end of your shift. For more information, refer to Finalizing and sending the tax return.

What comes next

Individuals may request an adjustment to their tax return. Be aware that although you are not expected to assist with adjustments as a CVITP volunteer, you may still choose to do so and are expected to keep the following information in mind:

  • Before any adjustments can be made, the individual must first wait to receive the notice of assessment for the year that needs adjustment
  • Generally, a change can be made to a tax return for a tax year ending in any of the ten previous calendar years (for example, a request made in 2021 cannot apply to a tax return dated earlier than the 2011)

Individuals may ask to make an adjustment to their tax return. As a CVITP volunteer you are not expected to assist with adjustments, however if you choose to assist the individual keep the following information in mind.

  • An individual must wait to receive their notice of assessment for the tax year needing a change before requesting the adjustment.
  • Generally, a change can be made to a return for a tax year ending in any of the ten previous calendar years. For example, a request made in 2021 must relate to the 2011 or a later tax year to be considered.

The individual must have:

  • their notice of assessment for the tax year requiring the adjustment
  • a copy of their tax return with all amounts reported
  • any receipts or tax slips supporting the adjustment

To request a change, the individual must complete, sign and date form T1-ADJ T1 Adjustment Request. Volunteers can choose to assist the individual but cannot act as their representative. Instructions on how to complete form T1-ADJ can be found on the back of the form itself.

If an individual wants to know when they can reasonably expect to receive their notice of reassessment, you can refer to Check CRA processing times for the most up-to-date information.

If an individual returns to the organization with follow-up questions about their return, refer them to the CRA’s Individual tax enquiries line, 1-800-959-8281. For any questions about tax refunds, refer them to Tax refunds: When to expect your refund.
