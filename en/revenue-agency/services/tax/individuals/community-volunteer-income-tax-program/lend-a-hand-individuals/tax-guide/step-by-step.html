Tax guide for volunteers

UFile’s interview setup

The interview setup includes sections related to tax situations. You may need to visit more than one section depending on the individual’s tax situation.

Find out how to report income and claim deductions, tax credits and expenses using the screen-by-screen guides within each situation.

Specific situations

Indigenous peoples, Newcomers, and incarcerated individuals.

Employment and other benefits

Employment income and employment insurance benefits, employment expenses, social assistance and workers’ compensation benefits.

Pension and other income

Pension income, Old age security, CPP, Registered pension, split pension income agreements, and other income reported on a T4A such as bursaries, RESP, RDSP and COVID-19 benefits.

Investment income

Interest income, and US social security.

Students

Tuition, education, textbook amounts, interest on student loans, and Canada training credit.

Common tax deductions

Medical expenses, disability tax credit, caregiver amounts, donations and RRSP.

Parents and children

Child care expenses, including special cases.

Other topics and left side menu

Residency information for tax administration agreements (Status Indian), Canada workers benefit advance payments, Self-employment and business income, Northern residents deduction, Controlling the claim for Climate action incentive payment (CAIP), Controlling the claim(s) for tax credits related to a dependant.
