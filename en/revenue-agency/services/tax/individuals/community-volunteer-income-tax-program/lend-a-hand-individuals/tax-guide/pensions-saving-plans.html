T4A(OAS)

  • enter the amounts from the individual’s T4A(OAS) slip into the corresponding fields

T4A(P) – Statement of Canada Pension Plan Benefits

  • enter the amounts from the individual’s T4A(P) slip into the corresponding fields

You are not required to key in the amount shown in box 20 of the T4A (P) slip. The software will automatically add it for you. 

T4A – Pension, retirement, annuity and other income

  • enter the amounts from the individual’s T4A slip into the corresponding fields
  • if the amounts from boxes 024 and 194 were received because the spouse is deceased, enter the amounts in the corresponding fields with the words – death of spouse in them
  • Fields 105, 131, 133, 146, 196, 197, 198, 199, 200, 201, 202, 203, 204, 205, and 211 can be found in the drop down menu, under Other information (COVID-19) benefits
  • Fields 144 and 146 can be found in the drop down menu under Footnotes relating to specific T-slip entries and Box 135
  • for box 201, enter the amount the individual wishes to claim for 2022

Splitting of pension income between spouses

  • leave Let MaxBack decide the selection to the question Do you wish to split eligible pension income with your spouse?

There is no need to do this step in the spouse or common-law partner’s file, the software will automatically generate form T1032 for the individual and their spouse or common-law partner.

  • print  form T1032 for each spouse, as they will need to sign each other’s copies and retain them for their records

T4RIF

  • enter the amounts from the individual’s T4RIF slip into the corresponding fields
  • if the amount from box 016 was received because the spouse is deceased, enter the amount in the field Taxable amounts from a RRIF upon death of a spouse

T4RSP

  • enter the amounts from the individual’s T4RSP slip into the corresponding fields
  • if the amount from box 016 was received because the spouse is deceased, enter the amount in the field 016 Annuity payments from RRSP - death of a spouse
