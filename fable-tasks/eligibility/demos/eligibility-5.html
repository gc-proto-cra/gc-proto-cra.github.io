  • Criteria 1 of 4: Someone else was paid to look after an eligible child so that you or the other person could do one of the following:

    • earn income from employment
    • carry on a business alone or as an active partner
    • carry on research or similar work for which you or the other person received a grant
    • attend school under the conditions of an educational program
      What is considered an educational program

      An educational program must be offered by a secondary school, college, university, or other designated educational institution.

      This includes any institution certified by the Minister of Employment and Social Development Canada for courses that develop or improve occupational skills. An eligible program must last at least three consecutive weeks.

      A full-time educational program requires students to spend at least 10 hours a week on courses or work in the program.

      A part-time educational program requires students to spend at least 12 hours in a calendar month on courses in the program.

  • Criteria 2 of 4: The child lived with you or the other person when the expenses were incurred

  • Criteria 3 of 4 Services were provided in Canada by a Canadian resident

    Exceptions

    If you lived outside Canada for all or part of 2025 and the CRA considers you to be a factual resident (opens in new window) or deemed resident (opens in new window) of Canada, you can claim child care expenses that you paid to a non-resident person for services provided outside Canada.

    For information on other situations where you can claim child care expenses paid for services provided outside Canada (for example, commuters to the United States), contact the Canada Revenue Agency (opens in new window).

    If you immigrated to or emigrated from Canada in 2025

    You can claim child care expenses for the period that you were in Canada, as long as you qualify.

  • Criteria 4 of 4:

    • You are the only person supporting the eligible child while they were living with you

    • Another person lived with you at any time in 2025 and in the first 60 days of 2026, and that person was one of the following:

      • the eligible child’s parent
      • your spouse or common-law partner, if you are the father or mother of the eligible child
      • an individual claiming an amount for the eligible child on their tax return
        Which amounts are included

        At least one of the following amounts are being claimed for the eligible child:

        • Amount for an eligible dependant (line 30400) (opens in new window)
        • Canada caregiver amount for spouse or common-law partner, or eligible dependant age 18 or older (line 30425) (opens in new window)
        • Canada caregiver amount for other infirm dependants age 18 or older (line 30450) (opens in new window)
        • Canada caregiver amount for infirm children under 18 years of age (line 30500) (opens in new window)
